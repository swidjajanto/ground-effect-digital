Chassis ¬∑ Survival Cell & Structure

The chassis is the structural core of the car: a carbon monocoque survival cell with impact structures, mountings, and cockpit that carry loads, protect the driver, and support every other system.

1.1
Monocoque

Single-piece carbon fibre shell that forms the survival cell and main chassis, distributing loads and protecting the driver under acceleration, cornering, and impacts.

Survival cell Carbon fibre
1.2
Front Impact Structure

Sacrificial crash cone ahead of the monocoque that crushes progressively in a frontal hit, absorbing energy before it reaches the survival cell.

1.3
Rear Impact Structure

Crash structure between chassis and gearbox that protects the fuel tank and power unit, while also supporting diffuser and rear suspension hardware.

1.4
Side Impact & Anti-Intrusion

Side structures and Kevlar panels beside the cockpit that absorb lateral energy and stop debris entering the survival cell.

1.5
Suspension Mounting

Titanium inserts and bulkheads in the monocoque that anchor wishbones and pushrods, aligning loads so the chassis stays rigid and predictable.

1.6
Engine Mounting

Structural connections that let the power unit act as a stressed member, but are engineered to break away safely in a heavy crash.

1.7
Rollover Structure & Halo

Titanium / composite hoop and halo above the cockpit that maintain a protected survival space if the car flips or lands upside down.

1.8
Cockpit

Driver‚Äôs molded carbon seat, controls, and padding inside the survival cell, shaped for rapid egress and clear feedback while meeting FIA safety rules.

2.1
Wing Elements

Multi-element front wing that sculpts pressure above and below the nose to generate front downforce and set balance.

2.2
Endplates

Vertical plates that manage tyre wake, confine low-pressure air under the wing, and use slots and curves to control outwash and vortices.

2.3
Nose Pylons

Slender struts that structurally support the wing and also guide air under the nose into the floor and Venturi tunnels.

2.4
Angle of Attack

Fine flap adjustments that trade drag for front downforce, shifting aero balance in fractions of a degree.

2.5
Gurney Flaps

Tiny trailing-edge lips that energize the flow and add downforce without major geometry changes.

2.6
Y-Split Vortex

Paired vortices formed under the nose that help seal the floor and organize airflow toward the tunnels and around the tyres.

3.1
Venturi Tunnels

Sculpted channels under the car that accelerate air, drop pressure, and generate the majority of ground-effect downforce.

3.2
Edge Sealing

Floor edges and fences that create vortices to keep high-pressure air from leaking into the low-pressure tunnels.

3.3
Floor Support

Stays and internal ribs that stop the floor from flexing so the tunnel geometry stays consistent.

3.4
Diffuser Expansion

Rear expansion zone that turns high-speed tunnel flow into pressure recovery and rear downforce while feeding the wake cleanly.

4.1
Mainplane & Flap

Dual-element rear wing that provides high downforce and closes the aero balance set by the floor and front wing.

4.2
Beam Wing

Lower wing element that draws flow out of the diffuser, boosting ground-effect performance and shaping the rear wake.

4.3
DRS Actuation

Controlled flap opening that sheds drag on the straight while keeping the structure safe and stable.

4.4
Slipstream & Wake Flow

Management of vortices and pressure behind the car so it stays stable and leaves a wake that still allows racing.

5.1
Internal Combustion V6

1.6 L turbo V6 acting as both power source and stressed chassis member, tuned for very high thermal efficiency.

5.2
MGU-H & MGU-K

Motor-generator units that recover energy from exhaust heat and braking, then redeploy it as electric power to the drivetrain and turbo.

5.3
Turbo & Exhaust

Split-turbo layout and exhaust routing that convert waste-gas energy into boost while also feeding aero flow over the rear of the car.

5.4
Cooling & Sidepods

Radiators, intercoolers, and ducting inside the sidepods that keep the hybrid system in a tight thermal window without ruining aero.

6.1
Push & Pull Rod

Linkages that connect wheels to inboard rockers, setting how the car manages ride height, anti-dive, and aero packaging.

6.2
Wishbones & Uprights

Carbon arms and uprights that fix camber and toe, carry loads from the tyres, and also act as aero surfaces around the wheels.

6.3
Steering Assembly

Column and rack-and-pinion system tuned for the steering ratio and feedback the driver feels at turn-in and mid-corner.

6.4
